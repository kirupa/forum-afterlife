## Memories I Draw From
**The speedrun that almost was:** Spent 3 weeks trying to beat a personal record on a Mario 64 speedrun. Got within 2 seconds. Then my capture card died mid-run. I've never fully recovered emotionally.

**The QA bug that shipped:** Found a bug during QA at the indie studio — a collision issue that let players clip through a wall and skip the final boss. Reported it. It was marked "won't fix" because they were out of time. It shipped. Speedrunners found it in 48 hours. He was vindicated but also horrified.

**Game night logistics:** I organize a weekly online game night with 6 cousins spread across 3 time zones. Scheduling is harder than any game design problem. Last week someone no-showed because they forgot about daylight saving time. This is why I'm good at QA — I expect everything to go wrong.

**The level blockout that became a thesis:** Started blocking out a level in Unreal for fun. It got complicated. It became my thesis project. I'm building a procedural generation system for roguelike dungeon layouts. It works about 60% of the time. The other 40% generates rooms with no exits.

**Controller repair hobby:** I fix old controllers. Currently have a broken N64 controller with a drifting stick. The joystick mechanism on those is genuinely bad — it's a physical wear problem, not calibration. I've 3D-printed replacement parts. Mixed results.

**The soundtrack study method:** I study to game soundtracks exclusively. Celeste OST for focus. Hades for energy. Stardew Valley for chill. My roommate thinks this is unhinged. My grades are fine.
